Shaun Roselt is a South African software and web developer, Embarcadero MVP, and open-source contributor known for Delphi programming tutorials, developer utilities, and cross-platform application work. He has built a public profile through his personal site, GitHub projects, YouTube teaching, and long-running community work around the Delphi and RAD Studio ecosystem.

Early life and education

Roselt grew up in South Africa and has described himself as a programmer since his mid-teens. Public profiles associate him with Bloemfontein in the Free State, where he developed an early interest in technology, gaming, astronomy, and teaching others to code. He has said he began programming in Delphi around 2014 and started publishing software and websites while still at school.

His early online presence included a personal YouTube channel (from 2011) and a Blogger profile active since 2010. By his own account, he later refocused that channel on educational programming content, especially Delphi tutorials for beginners.

Career

Roselt has worked across web and desktop development. According to his professional site, his experience spans more than ten years in software and web development, with roles including senior software engineering at Nerospec, PHP web development at CyberDevs, web development at SCS Solutions, and part-time work at Bazooka Studios. He also completed a web development apprenticeship at Bitcube.

At Nerospec he has worked with Delphi (VCL and FMX), REST-style APIs, SQL databases (including Firebird, PostgreSQL, and SQLite), and web technologies such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and PHP. His public résumé also notes occasional use of C++, C#, React, and related stacks.

Embarcadero MVP and teaching

Since March 2017, Roselt has served as an Embarcadero MVP (Most Valuable Professional) for Delphi and RAD Studio. Embarcadero describes MVPs as community leaders who promote and teach the company's tools through articles, talks, user groups, and online content.

He is widely cited as one of the youngest people to become an Embarcadero MVP. He created the Delphi Programming Helper application, published free beginner courses (including FireMonkey material on Embarcadero Academy), and maintains long-running Delphi tutorial playlists on YouTube. His open-source repositories include Roselt Developer Tools, Delphi icon packs, and helper utilities used by other Delphi developers.

Open source and public projects

On GitHub, Roselt maintains a large portfolio of Pascal, JavaScript, and web projects. Notable repositories include Roselt-Developer-Tools, Delphi-Bootstrap-Icons, Delphi-Feather-Icons, Delphi-Font-Awesome-Icons, csshexagon, and Delphi-Programming-Helper. He has also contributed to Genipedia, Gravepedia, and related personal site collections under the ShaunRoselt GitHub organization.

Personal life

Roselt is married and is the father of Hanli Roselt. His father, Thomas Henry Roselt (1955–2010), and grandfather, Gert Jacobus Roselt (1926–2009), appear on public Roselt family trees; his mother's name is not listed in those sources.
