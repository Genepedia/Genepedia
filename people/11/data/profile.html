Elon Musk

Elon Reeve Musk (born 28 June 1971) is a businessman and entrepreneur known for his leadership of electric vehicle manufacturer Tesla, space launch company SpaceX, social media platform X (formerly Twitter), and artificial intelligence company xAI. Born in Pretoria, South Africa, he emigrated to Canada and later the United States, where he built a series of technology companies that reshaped online payments, commercial rocketry, electric transportation, and public discourse on the internet. He has repeatedly ranked among the wealthiest people in the world, primarily through his equity stakes in Tesla and SpaceX.

Musk co-founded the web software company Zip2 in the 1990s and sold it to Compaq in 1999. He then founded X.com, an online financial services company that merged with Confinity to become PayPal; eBay acquired PayPal in 2002. With proceeds from that sale, Musk founded SpaceX in 2002 with the stated goal of reducing space transportation costs and enabling human settlement of Mars. He joined Tesla in 2004 as chairman and later became chief executive officer, helping popularise battery-electric cars and expand the company's energy storage business. His other ventures include Neuralink (brain–computer interfaces), the Boring Company (tunnel construction), and xAI (artificial intelligence).

Early life and family

Musk was born in Pretoria to Maye Musk, a model and dietitian, and Errol Musk, an electromechanical engineer, pilot, and property developer. He has a younger brother, Kimbal Musk, who became a restaurateur and entrepreneur, and a younger sister, Tosca Musk, a filmmaker. His parents divorced when he was young; Musk has described a difficult childhood and a complicated relationship with his father.

As a child Musk was an avid reader and developed an early interest in computing. At age twelve he sold his first software—a video game called Blastar—to a computer magazine. He attended Waterkloof House Preparatory School and Bryanston High School before graduating from Pretoria Boys High School. Musk has said he was bullied during his school years. To avoid mandatory military service in apartheid-era South Africa, he left for Canada in 1989, using his mother's Canadian citizenship to obtain a passport.

Education and move to North America

Musk spent two years at Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario, then transferred to the University of Pennsylvania, where he earned bachelor's degrees in economics from the Wharton School and physics from the College of Arts and Sciences in 1997. He moved to California to pursue a PhD in applied physics at Stanford University but left after two days to join the internet boom, founding Zip2 with his brother Kimbal and Greg Kuriakose.

Zip2 and PayPal era

Zip2 provided online city guide software for newspapers, including maps and business directories. Compaq acquired the company in 1999 for approximately $307 million, of which Musk received about $22 million. He used part of that capital to co-found X.com, one of the first federally insured online banks. X.com merged with Confinity, which operated PayPal, and the combined company adopted the PayPal brand. Musk was briefly CEO before being replaced amid internal disputes; he remained the largest shareholder until eBay bought PayPal in 2002 for $1.5 billion in stock, netting Musk roughly $175 million.

SpaceX

Musk founded Space Exploration Technologies Corp. (SpaceX) in 2002 in Hawthorne, California. Frustrated by the high cost of rockets, he sought to develop reusable launch vehicles. After three failed launches of the Falcon 1, SpaceX successfully reached orbit in 2008—the first privately funded liquid-fuel rocket to do so. That success helped the company secure NASA contracts, including Commercial Resupply Services for the International Space Station.

SpaceX developed the Falcon 9, Falcon Heavy, and Dragon spacecraft, and later the fully reusable Starship launch system intended for deep-space missions. The company has launched thousands of Starlink satellites to provide satellite internet service worldwide. In 2020, SpaceX's Crew Dragon became the first private spacecraft to carry NASA astronauts to the ISS. SpaceX has also won contracts for lunar lander development under NASA's Artemis program. Musk serves as CEO and chief technology officer and has stated that making humanity a multiplanetary species is his long-term objective.

Tesla

Musk led Tesla's initial investment round in 2004 and became chairman of the board. After the 2008 financial crisis, he took a more active operational role and became CEO in 2008. Under his leadership Tesla launched the Roadster, Model S, Model X, Model 3, Model Y, and Cybertruck, while building a global network of Supercharger stations and battery Gigafactories. The company also sells solar products and energy storage systems through Tesla Energy.

Tesla went public in 2010 and later joined the S&P 500. Its market capitalisation surged in the early 2020s as electric vehicle adoption accelerated. Musk's compensation has included large performance-based stock option packages tied to market value and operational milestones. He has promoted over-the-air software updates, autonomous driving research, and the Optimus humanoid robot programme. Critics have raised concerns about production targets, Autopilot safety, labour practices, and Musk's public statements about the company.

SolarCity, OpenAI, and other ventures

In 2006 Musk co-founded SolarCity, a solar energy services company led by his cousins; Tesla acquired SolarCity in 2016 in a controversial all-stock deal that expanded Tesla's energy business. Musk co-founded OpenAI in 2015 as a nonprofit AI research laboratory; he departed the board in 2018 and later criticised its direction toward commercialisation and partnership with Microsoft. He founded Neuralink in 2016 to develop implantable brain–machine interfaces, and the Boring Company in 2016 to construct tunnels for transportation systems such as the Las Vegas Convention Center loop.

In 2023 Musk founded xAI to build artificial intelligence systems, releasing the Grok chatbot integrated with X. He has also been involved in Hyperloop concept promotion, though the idea has been pursued mainly by independent companies.

Acquisition of Twitter and X

In April 2022 Musk agreed to acquire Twitter for approximately $44 billion, taking the company private. After attempting to terminate the deal, he completed the purchase in October 2022. He renamed the platform X in 2023 and implemented sweeping changes: mass layoffs, subscription features, revised content moderation policies, and the restoration of previously suspended accounts. The acquisition was financed partly through Tesla stock sales and significant debt loaded onto the company.

Musk's management of X drew praise from some users who favoured looser moderation and criticism from advertisers, civil society groups, and former employees concerned about hate speech, misinformation, and platform stability. He appointed CEOs to run X at times but remained deeply involved in product and policy decisions.

Political activity and public role

Musk's political views have shifted over time. He has donated to both Democratic and Republican candidates and has become an increasingly prominent voice on U.S. politics and culture war issues through X. In 2024 he endorsed Donald Trump for president and spent heavily to support his campaign. In early 2025 he led the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) advisory effort in the second Trump administration, focusing on federal spending cuts and bureaucratic restructuring—a role that attracted intense public and legal scrutiny.

Musk's posts on X frequently move financial markets and news cycles. Supporters describe him as a visionary industrialist willing to take extreme risks; detractors cite labour disputes, regulatory clashes, erratic statements, and conflicts of interest between his companies and government roles.

Personal life

Musk married Canadian author Justine Musk in 2000; they divorced in 2008. He married English actress Talulah Riley twice (2010–2012 and 2013–2016). He had a relationship with musician Grimes (Claire Boucher), with whom he has three children, and with Neuralink executive Shivon Zilis, with whom he has twins. Musk has at least twelve publicly known children. His daughter Vivian Jenna Wilson, formerly Xavier Musk, legally changed her name and gender in 2022 and has publicly distanced herself from him.

Musk has described himself as having Asperger syndrome (now considered part of the autism spectrum). He has cited long working hours across his companies and has lived in Texas after moving from California, partly in response to that state's policies and taxes.

Honours, wealth, and recognition

  • Time Person of the Year (2021)
  • Fellow of the Royal Society (2018)
  • Honorary doctorate in engineering and technology, Yale University (2015)
  • Repeatedly ranked at or near the top of global wealth indices, driven by Tesla and SpaceX valuations
  • Led SpaceX to the first commercial crewed orbital flight by a private company (2020)

Controversies and legal matters

Musk has faced numerous lawsuits and regulatory actions. The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission sued him in 2018 over tweets claiming he had "funding secured" to take Tesla private; Musk settled, paid fines, and temporarily stepped down as Tesla chairman while remaining CEO. He has been involved in defamation cases, labour complaints at Tesla factories, FAA and environmental reviews of SpaceX launch sites, and disputes over the Twitter acquisition and X content policies.

His statements on COVID-19, cryptocurrency, geopolitics, and individual journalists have drawn widespread criticism. Biographers and investigative reporters have documented patterns of ambitious goal-setting, intense management style, and personal behaviour that polarise public opinion. Despite controversy, his companies remain central to electric vehicles, commercial spaceflight, and online political discourse in the twenty-first century.
